Automatic Dialogue Generation with Expressed Emotions

Abstract
Despite myriad efforts in the literature designing neural dialogue NLP term generation systems NLP term in recent years, very few consider putting restrictions on the response itself. They learn from collections of past responses and generate one based on a given utterance NLP term without considering, speech act, desired style NLP term or emotion NLP term to be expressed. In this research, we address the problem of forcing the dialogue generation NLP term to express emotion NLP term . We present three models NLP term that either concatenate the desired emotion NLP term with the source input NLP term during the learning, or push the emotion NLP term in the decoder NLP term . The results, evaluated with an emotion NLP term tagger, are encouraging with all three models NLP term , but present better outcome and promise with our model NLP term that adds the emotion vector NLP term in the decoder NLP term .
1 Introduction
Automatic dialogue generation NLP term (Ritter et al., 2011) aims at generating human-like NLP term responses given a human-to-human dialogue NLP term history. Most conversational agents NLP term are specialized for a specific domain NLP term such as travel booking (Xu and Rudnicky, 2000) and are typically finite state-based or template-based. Open domain NLP term dialogue systems NLP term have seen a growing interest in recent years thanks to neural dialogue NLP term generation systems NLP term , based on deep learning NLP term models NLP term . These systems do not encode dialog NLP term structure and are entirely data NLP term -driven. They learn to predict the maximum-likelihood estimation (MLE) based on a large training NLP term corpus NLP term . The machine learning NLP term -based system basically learns to predict the words and the sentence NLP term to respond based on the previous utterances NLP term . However, while such a system can generate grammatically correct NLP term and human-like NLP term answers, the responses are often generic and non-committal instead of being specific and emotionally NLP term intelligent. For instance, we can not dictate a particular emotion NLP term to express.

In this paper, we consider a model NLP term in which the wished emotion NLP term to be expressed is injected to direct the response generation NLP term . For example, if the user says: “I just missed my deadline.” If we want the system to respond with sadness NLP term , it could be “I am sorry to hear that.”, but we can also force the response to express anger NLP term : “You should never do it again!”
There are some challenges to tackle this task.
• The current neural dialogue NLP term models NLP term are not satisfactory in general.
• There is a lack of dialogue NLP term corpora NLP term that are labeled with emotions NLP term .
• The evaluation NLP term is hard because emotion NLP term is subjective and sometimes ambiguous NLP term .
The idea is to use an emotion NLP term mining from text classifier NLP term (Yadollahi et al., 2017) to predict the emotion NLP term or emotions NLP term expressed in the source utterance NLP term , then decide based on the detected emotions NLP term , which emotion NLP term e is expressed in the response. The response is evaluated using the same emotion classifier NLP term and is declared successful if e is predicted from the response. The emotion NLP term tagger we use is based on the work in (Yadollahi et al., 2017) but uses a deep learning NLP term model NLP term and trains on 9 emotions NLP term : anger NLP term , disgust NLP term , fear NLP term , guilt NLP term , joy, love NLP term , sadness NLP term , surprise NLP term , and thankfulness. These are based on the six basic emotions NLP term from Ekman’s model NLP term (Ekman, 1992), to which we added guilt NLP term , love NLP term and thankfulness in the context of an open ended conversational agent NLP term that we aim to be emotionally NLP term intelligent for companionship to elderly users.
In this paper, we proposed three approaches to make our model NLP term of our conversational agent NLP term generate responses expressing specific emotions NLP term . The first two approaches add the emotion NLP term as a token NLP term with the input NLP term during the learning either before the utterance NLP term sentence NLP term or after, and the third approach injects the desired emotion NLP term directly in the decoder NLP term .
2 Related Work
Vinyals and Le (2015) adopted the Sequence-tosequence (Seq2Seq) model NLP term used in machine translation NLP term (Sutskever et al., 2014) in the task of automatic response generation NLP term . Seq2Seq learns to generate a sequence NLP term of words from another sequence NLP term of words as input NLP term . Since then, many works based on this framework NLP term have been conducted to improve the response quality from different points of view. Reinforcement learning has also been adopted to force the model NLP term to have longer discussions (Li et al., 2016b). Serban et al. (2017) proposed a hierarchical NLP term encoder NLP term to generate a response from more utterances NLP term . Moreover, there are also attempts to avoid generating dull, short responses (Li et al., 2017a,b).

7 Conclusion
Emotional intelligence is the ability to monitor interlocutor’s emotions NLP term and in turn appropriately express emotions NLP term in response. In our case, monitoring emotions NLP term in utterances NLP term is done using an emotion NLP term mining classifier NLP term . We assume that given some mapping NLP term rules, we can decide to express a specific emotion NLP term in the response. For instance if the message expresses sadness NLP term , the response could express compassion or surprise NLP term depending upon context. The work presented herein focuses solely on generating a response that expresses a given desired emotion NLP term , and assumes the emotion NLP term to be expressed is given via these mapping NLP term rules. In this paper, we show that it is indeed possible to generate fluent responses that express a desired emotion NLP term . We present three models NLP term to do so. Despite the differences among the models NLP term , they are all trained towards minimizing − log p(Y |X, e) and all converge. We believe that adding reasoning to the mix can further enhance the emotional intelligence of a conversational agent NLP term .
